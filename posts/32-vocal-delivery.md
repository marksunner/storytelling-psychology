# It's Not What You Say, It's the Way You Say It

---

Professional vocal skills and tonality can significantly elevate the delivery of any message, talk, or presentation. Good vocal skills involve clear enunciation, proper pacing, and appropriate volume. These factors help to ensure that the audience can easily understand and engage with the speaker's message.

---

## The Power of Tonality

In addition to clear enunciation and pacing, tonality — or the emotional quality of the speaker's voice — is also crucial in the delivery of a message. Tonality can convey emotion, add emphasis, and even change the meaning of words. For example, a speaker may use a higher pitch to convey excitement or a lower pitch to convey gravitas.

Effective use of tonality can help to engage and connect with the audience on a deeper level. A speaker who is able to convey genuine emotion through their voice is more likely to be perceived as authentic and credible. In contrast, a speaker who lacks tonality or uses a monotone delivery may come across as unengaged or uninterested, leading to a disconnect with the audience.

---

## Voice as Personality

In addition to engaging the audience, good vocal skills and tonality can also help to convey the speaker's personality and charisma. A speaker with a warm, engaging voice is more likely to be perceived as likeable and approachable, while a speaker with a cold, monotone voice may be perceived as unapproachable or even unprofessional.

Effective vocal skills and tonality are especially important in public speaking and presentations, where the speaker may not have the benefit of visual aids or body language to convey their message. In these situations, the speaker's voice becomes the primary means of communication, making it even more crucial to use it effectively.

---

## Three Basic Vocal Techniques

**1. Use varied pitch and inflection**

Varying the pitch and inflection of your voice can add interest and emphasis to your speech. For example, raising the pitch of your voice on key words or phrases can add excitement or emphasis, while using a lower pitch can convey gravitas or seriousness.

**2. Use pauses and silence effectively**

Pauses and silence can be powerful tools in public speaking. They can create suspense, allow the audience to absorb information, and give the speaker time to gather their thoughts. However, it's important to use them judiciously and not overuse them, as too many pauses can disrupt the flow of the speech.

**3. Use vocal variety**

Vocal variety refers to the range of sounds and tones that a speaker can produce with their voice. Using a range of vocal qualities, such as volume, pitch, and inflection, can help to keep the audience engaged and add interest to the speech. To achieve vocal variety, try speaking in different octaves, and varying the pace and rhythm of your speech.

---

## Conclusion

Professional vocal skills and tonality can significantly elevate the delivery of any message, talk, or presentation. Whether it's conveying emotion, adding emphasis, or engaging the audience, the way a speaker says something is often just as important as what they say. It is the combination of what is said and how it is said that really moves people and affects them on a deeper level.
