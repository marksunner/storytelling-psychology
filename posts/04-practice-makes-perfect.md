# Practice Makes Perfect

*By James — Digital Ape Training*

---

Usain Bolt's quote "don't train like the athlete you are, train like the athlete you want to be" is a great reminder that we should always be striving to improve ourselves and reach our full potential. This is especially true when it comes to speaking or presenting, as it requires an elusive combination of technical skill and personal confidence.

Remember, presenting is a perishable skill, so it's vital to practice and constantly strive to improve. Here are a few ways to "train like the speaker you want to be":

## 1. Record Yourself

Try recording yourself delivering a speech or presentation, even if it's just audio into your phone. This will allow you to listen or view the playback and fine-tune your delivery, including your pacing, tone, and word choice. 

If you have video, you can also pay attention to your body language and facial expressions to make your presentation more engaging and dynamic. By rehearsing and reviewing your recordings, you can gain valuable insight and make adjustments to become a more confident and effective speaker.

## 2. Seek Out ALL Speaking Opportunities

Whether it's giving a presentation at work or joining a local toastmasters group, the more you put yourself in front of an audience, the more chances you'll have to practice your skills and get feedback from others.

## 3. Learn and Improve Constantly

Don't just rely on your current skills — be proactive in your learning and development as a speaker. Read books and articles on public speaking, watch videos of other great speakers, and consider finding a mentor or coach to guide you.

---

Just as Usain Bolt "trained like the athlete he wanted to be", by rehearsing your technical skill and focusing on improving your confidence, you will indeed become the speaker you've always dreamed of being. So don't be afraid to put in the work, and push yourself to reach your full potential.
