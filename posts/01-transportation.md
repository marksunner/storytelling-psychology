# Transportation

*By Mark Sunner — Digital Ape Training*

---

Here is a weird thought that might give you pause — have you ever stopped to consider that your reality is actually fiction?

Although we rarely stop to consider it, we are not connected to the outside world at all — instead we are all essentially living in a controlled hallucination, trapped inside the dark bone vault of our own skulls. As the single point of focus for all of our senses and experiences, it is easy to see how our conscious experience can be influenced by external stimuli. Sight, sound, smell, touch, and taste are all just interpretations that are transformed into thoughts and actions, only some of which become memories.

However, when we are exposed to a compelling narrative, this process can be hijacked. A good story has the ability to transport us to another world, fully immersing us in the experience. Our bodies respond as if the events are happening to us in real life, with heart rates increasing, blood vessels dilating, and neurochemicals influencing our emotional state. This phenomenon, known as **transportation**, can be seen when we become so engrossed in a book that we forget our surroundings or lose track of time.

Psychologists have found that transportation can alter our beliefs, attitudes, and intentions, and these changes can persist even after the experience has ended. This demonstrates the power of a skilled storyteller to persuade and influence their audience.

In light of this, it is important for presenters to focus on the storytelling aspect of their presentations rather than solely on technical descriptions or chronological details. Using vivid language and creating a sense of immersion can help to transport the audience, making the message more impactful and memorable.

Unique access to the controlled hallucination of the mind highlights the importance of storytelling in communication. By captivating the audience and fully immersing them in the experience, we are able to persuade and influence them in a way that goes beyond mere facts and figures.

## Three Transportation Techniques

Next time you deliver a speech or presentation, why not try incorporating some transportation techniques into your delivery? Here are three ideas you might like to try:

1. **Use vivid language and descriptive detail to create a sense of immersion:** By painting a vivid and detailed picture of the story or experience, you can help the audience to fully engage with the narrative and feel as if they are a part of it.

2. **Build a strong emotional connection with the audience:** Transportation is often facilitated by strong emotions, so try to evoke feelings of excitement, fear, joy, or any other strong emotions through your storytelling.

3. **Create a sense of credibility and authenticity:** If the audience believes that the story or experience is real, they are more likely to be transported. To do this, you can use real-life examples, incorporate sensory details, and use language that is authentic and believable.
