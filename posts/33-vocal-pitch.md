---

Pitch is an important aspect of professional speaking that can greatly affect the impact and effectiveness of your delivery. By understanding and utilising pitch variance, you can add depth, emotion, and musicality to your voice, making your message more engaging and compelling for your audience.

---

## The Emotional Range of Pitch

One way to use pitch is to vary your pitch to reflect the emotional charge of your message. For example, if you are describing something in a positive light, you may want to use a higher pitch to convey excitement and enthusiasm. On the other hand, if you are describing something in a negative light, you may want to use a lower pitch to convey a sense of seriousness or concern.

---

## The Seesaw Metaphor

Conceptually, we can think of the 'up/down' of pitch variance as being a bit like a seesaw: Imagine for a moment a seesaw with only one person on it — it never went up, or down, but just stayed put in one place — this would be the most boring seesaw in the world! Unfortunately, this is also exactly what it would be like to listen to someone whose voice is entirely monotone.

By deliberately expanding the use of pitch, our audience will be naturally drawn in by the rhythm and musicality in your voice, which can help keep them engaged and make your message more memorable. Some of the most popular narrators on audiobook sites, such as Audible, are known for their ability to use pitch to add musicality to their voices, making their narration far more enjoyable to listen to.

---

## Improving Your Pitch

To improve your pitch delivery, it is a good idea to record yourself and listen back for any patches of monotone or flat delivery. You may also want to seek feedback from others and practice your pitch in different situations to build confidence and improve your delivery.

---

## Three Tips for Better Pitch

**1. Utilise pitch variance to reflect emotional valence**

By varying the pitch of your voice to convey different emotions, you can increase the depth and impact of your message. For instance, you may use a higher pitch to convey excitement and enthusiasm, and a lower pitch to convey a sense of gravity or concern.

**2. Employ inflection to add emphasis**

Inflection refers to the way you alter the pitch of your voice to convey meaning or emphasis. By using inflection strategically, you can make certain words or phrases stand out and add emphasis to your message.

**3. Practice frequently**

The most effective way to improve your use of pitch when public speaking is to practice regularly. This will help you build confidence and become more comfortable using different pitches and inflection to convey your message. You can practice by recording yourself and listening back, seeking feedback from others, and practicing in various contexts.

---

## Conclusion

Pitch is a powerful tool to enhance the effectiveness of your speaking. By understanding and utilising pitch variance, you can add depth and emotion to your voice, making your message more engaging and memorable for any audience. Remember to vary your pitch to reflect the emotional charge of your message and to create a sense of rhythm and musicality, and avoid using a monotone voice. With practice and awareness, you can improve your pitch delivery and become a more engaging and memorable speaker.
